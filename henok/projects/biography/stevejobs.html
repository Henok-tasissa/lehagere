Steve Job's Contribution and Impact on
Technology in Modern Society

Steve was born in a family not related to technology and management. He was fond of electronics from young age because his after taught him basic about electronics. His first invention was electronic card despite the weakness in his education. He did not achieve success in the study and he took a job as a game designer to collect money for his travel. He traveled to India at the beginning of his youth and then returned from his trip to United States. He began inventing laptop at very early stages of his life and then "Apple" has become the largest company in the world in a very short time. This has changed the technology drastically and it has changed the world with the technology. Steve jobs provided an easier and smaller device than it was available before and after that he invented Mac and iPad.

He has a major contribution on modern education because he had a visionary prescription to revolutionize teaching and learning. He wanted to revolutionize the education in the same way he did for music, phones and computers. He wanted to see the technology available in every classroom. He break the teacher unions and give parents and students vouchers to choose their school, make learning more holistic and attract new generation that could rethink learning experience. Today his legacy lives on with his educational apps and iPads. He has made learning exceptional with his lifelong dedication.

Steve Jobs, has changed the way people communicates, interacts and entertain. He has even changed the way we work. He has created user friendly product that are combatable for both user and business. He is a good inspiration to all human beings. He had an aim of creating something that will touch a lot of people and put them to work either directly or indirectly. So he has done something which entrepreneurs can only dream of achieving. When it comes to digital media, no products have been more influential than the iPod and iTunes and Steve Jobs lead the development of both. He has a big contribution in reanimating the film industry.
